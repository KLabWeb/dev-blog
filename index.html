Some Thoughts


Life is short. Its comes and goes in the blink of an eye. Every day is an opportunity. Along with spending close time with those I care for, from what I've found, the best use of this limited time comes from endeavors that focus on novel experiences, learning, and exploration, Pursuit of a passion and craft is one way to achieve this.

For much of my 20â€™s I hopped between different tech jobs, from Network Operations Center Admin to contract programmer for hire. After experiencing a wide array of what I had learned in college, now in the workforce, and during my first developer job, I confirmed what I had figured all along; programming is where I want to be.

But what to focus on? Artificial intelligence and machine learning for fintech? Or maybe I should use my past experience in networking and systems in systems applications development. Thinking on it, what I wanted most off all, was more full stack, as most of my work prior had been back-end. And for this reason, I chose web development.

Through web development, I had not just the basics of CSS, HTML, and JavaScript, for static sites, but many frameworks and libraries, such as Vue, React, and Angular. And so I learned HTML, then CSS, then JavaScript, and finally React. At my current job I use all of the above, as well as PHP, jQuery, and Bootstrap.

But after coding for a year in a largely procedural legacy PHP codebase, seemingly built with very little to no design in mind, the time for a change is long overdue. And like most things, I think this means more study of the foundations. Having now worked in Python, PHP, Java, C, JavaScript, SQL, and COBOL, languages are quick to learn. Its the foundations, theory, principles, adaptability, experience, and clean code and architecture that truly make a programmer.

So now, I am largely focused on OO design patterns, which while I have learned and used in the past, have not worked with for years. The other part of that is paradigms, specifically functional, object oriented, and procedural. Functional and OO are where I want to be, so I am giving them focus. For languages, frameworks, and libraries; Python, Django, MongoDB, React, and Laravel.

The more I learn, the more I practice, the more skilled I become, the better programmer I can be. The most important thing though, is the will to do so, the persistent drive to keep learning, growing, and seeking, and always analyzing to see what I can improve on and how I can do it. This blog is a timeline of such tasks.
