Some Thoughts


Life is short. Its comes and goes in the blink of an eye. Every day is an opportunity. Along with spending close time with those I care for, from what I've found, the best use of this limited time comes from endeavors that focus on learning, exploration, art, and challenge, as well as never getting caught in a bubble for too long. Pursuit of a passion and craft is one way to achieve this.

For much of my twenties I hopped between different types tech jobs, from Network Operations Center Admin to contract programmer for hire. After experiencing a wide array of focuses, once getting my first developer job, I confirmed what I had guessed all along; programming is where I want to be.

But what to focus on? Artificial intelligence and machine learning for fintech? Or maybe I should use my past experience in networking and systems in systems applications development. Thinking on it, what I wanted most off all, was more full stack, as most of my work prior had been back-end. And for this reason, I chose web development.

Through web development, I had a vast assortment of languages and technologies to work with and learn, from the basics of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, to back ends in languages like PHP and Python, and then of course the front end, with libraries and frameworks like React, Vue, and Angular.

And on top of that, along the way, many other additions, like Laravel, Django, and Flask, design patterns and principles, REST & GraphQL, components & modularization, microservices, algorithms and data structures, databases, Agile, DevOps; the list of skills and technologies I could master went on and on.

But now, after my previous job, coding for a year in a largely procedural, legacy PHP codebase, seemingly built with very little to no design in mind, the time for a change was long overdue. And like most things, this took me back to the foundations, focusing on studying new languages and personal projects. Having now worked in Python, PHP, Java, C, JavaScript, SQL, and COBOL, languages are quick to learn. So, in addition to my language and tech studies, I decided to devote much more time also to theory, design principles and patterns, modularization, functional programming, and the clean code and architecture that truly make a programmer.

And thus comes the current state of my development work; a mix of personal projects and studies. On the theory side, I am giving a greater focus to object oriented design principles and patterns and SOLID, DRY development, as well as functional programming on the front end. Language wise, I am currently working on advanced Python and modern PHP, with Flask, Django, and more Laravel to follow. On the front end, I'm expanding my React knowledge, with hooks. In addition to this, I'm doing a bit of DevOps, learning Docker Compose, Swarm, and Stack, to be followed by Kubernetes. It's a lot to focus on, but by studying in well documented and iterative sprints, I am managing it well.

The more I learn, the more I practice, the more skilled I become, the better programmer I can be. The most important thing though, is the will to do so, the persistent drive to keep learning, growing, and seeking, and always analyzing to see what I can improve on or modify, and how I can do it. This blog is a timeline of such tasks.
