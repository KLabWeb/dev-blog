Some Thoughts

"The true delight is in the finding out rather than in the knowing," Issac Assimov

Since a young age I have spent much of my time consumed by computers. In elementary school, during the days of 26k internet, my family was lucky enough to win a Compaq Presario from a radio contest. The result was a now long lost GeoCities movie review site. In early high school I built my first computer with money saved from working a grocery store job and set up one of the disks as storage for an FTP server for my neighborhood friends to share music, movies, and ISOs on. In college I expanded my formal education, and since graduating I have worked a variety of technology jobs, from NOC to mobile application development.

This site is my current endeavor and a very welcome return to programming. One of my core beliefs is that if you a truly passionate about something, you are never done learning it. For me, the pursuit, the chase, is always the most enjoyable aspect of my ventures. By constantly learning and exploring new skills, ways of thought, passions, and modes of action, the joyous experience of inquiry and uncovering never has to end. This is major part of my worldview in general; people, places, hobbies, explorations; indeed much of my life revolves around these ideas.

In this blog I hope to display some of my passion, work, and quest for knowledge, diversity, and experience. Through it I will be exploring HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript ES9, React.js, functional programming, SASS, MySql, Node.js, Express, Python, RDBM, Django, and what it means to create beautiful and truthful code. Three cheers to brain plasticity. Enjoy.
